This project focuses on analyzing epilepsy seizure patterns using a structured, end‑to‑end Python workflow. The goal was to explore how factors such as sleep deviation, medication adherence, and demographic characteristics relate to seizure frequency. I began by preparing a cleaned dataset, ensuring that the data was consistent, structured, and ready for analysis. Using Pandas, I explored trends over time, examined variable relationships, and prepared the data for visualization.

I then used Seaborn and Matplotlib to create a series of clear, interpretable visuals, including seizure trends over time, a correlation heatmap of numeric variables, a scatter plot comparing sleep deviation and seizure count, and a demographic breakdown by age group. These visuals helped highlight meaningful patterns, such as how sleep irregularities may relate to seizure activity and how seizure frequency varies across age groups.

The project demonstrates my ability to work with real‑world data, build reproducible analysis scripts, and communicate insights visually. It also reflects my growing proficiency with Python’s data analysis ecosystem and my commitment to developing strong analytical workflows that can support data‑driven decision‑making.
